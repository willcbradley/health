Here Are The Role Models

Medicine is timeless.

No matter what superficial career games are layered on top of it, the fundamentals remain the same. People still get sick, and we still need to help them.

It's tempting to think of ourselves as special. To believe that the universal principles don't apply to our situation, because we're different. I'm certainly guilty of this when it comes to medicine — acting as though my circumstances were unique, and my problems were unsolvable.

Of course, these are excuses.

We can learn the fundamentals of medicine, regardless of circumstances. And if we get through most of those, we can branch out into adjacent fields. We can become fundamentally valuable, no matter what.

No amount of job shortages, or industry politics, or nepotism, or exams, or legislative changes can stop us.

Nobody is holding us back. Nobody was ever really holding us back. This is a terrifying weight of responsibility, but also a huge opportunity.

There are no excuses any more.

We're free.

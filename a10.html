Leap Of Faith

Medicine is competitive.

It selects for people who are great at standardised testing — great at following instructions, regardless of what those instructions might be.

This never sat well with me. If being a medical student is largely about following instructions, why strive? Who in their right mind would work to become the best at unquestioning rule-following? Why not spend time working out which rules to follow in the first place?

This was my approach for the first few years.

Instead of going above and beyond within the curriculum, I poured all my spare time into trying to figure out foundational ideas, principles, that could always be relied on. I figured out how to operate in the world, and why to do anything in the first place (at least, as far as I can tell). And now, it's time to put these principles into practice.

To do this, I need to start developing real (rather than theoretical) competence, and medicine seems like a perfectly good place to start.

But this brings us back to the original problem: why work to become the best rule-follower? I've figured out all these principles — what separates me, practically, from someone who mindlessly completes whatever task it put in front of them?

Nothing.

That's the terrifying part. We can't put principles into practice if we aren't practicing. To apply our own rules to medicine, we first need to master the existing ones, even though they're externally imposed.

It's a leap of faith. We need to risk complete mediocrity (or worse, complete failure) before we can become something spectacular.

And worst of all, failing to adopt these constraints ensures that we remain extremely mediocre (at best) forever. We get all the downside of external rules, with none of the upside of our own capabilities. Maximum pain, no reward. Nothing could be worse than that.

But if we jump? We have a shot at getting the best of both worlds. Grounding ourselves in the disciplinary structure of medicine, and branching out to solve problems nobody else has even thought of yet. Combining the best insights of the past with the best of our own to build something incredible.

I've read a lot about the psychology of creativity. Largely, it seems like a curse. But if we can make it through the additional pain, if we can pay the higher cost of admission, creativity can make us unstoppable.

Time won't wait for us. The ground is dissolving beneath our feet. Either we take the leap now, or we'll be pushed off toward certain death.

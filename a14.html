Demoralisation

Strength is perishable.

When we're sheltered by status games, we grow weak. And weakness has a way of spreading.

Over time, all real feedback mechanisms are replaced with arbitrary rules — rules that inevitably become corrupt due to separation from reality.

The natural extreme is maximal corruption: systems that punish us for doing good, and reward us for doing bad.

Worse still, people build their lives on these distorted systems. They stake their careers, or reputations, or relationships on the hope that things will stay the same, and as such, they're inventicised to actively enforce broken rules.

Why does this matter?

Because corrupt status games leave us in an incredibly demoralising scenario. Either we genuinely strive forward (which the world punishes us for), or we resign ourselves to doing fake (perhaps even actively harmful) work.

This has largely been my experience of medicine so far. I refuse to believe all hope is lost.

Non-Pathetic Doctors

Dependence creates vulnerability.

For as long as we need anything from somebody, they have complete leverage over us.

(There are situations where this is arguably appropriate, but our professional lives aren't one of them).

Why is this relevant?

We can't stop corrupt status games from forming. We can't stop them from trying to demoralise their inhabitants. But we can change our dependence on them.

By building from fundamentals, we can work our way out of the status game.

With enough effort, our mere existence makes a mockery of the corruption. Once the punishments of the status game carry less weight, we're free to do real work.

What does this mean?

For the first few years of medical school, I've been extremely demoralised.

It seemed like the options were to (1) play into the demoralising drudgery of the status game, or (2) go into the markets, and get eaten alive by the pace of technological change.

But we've just established that there are other options.

We can get the upside of status games, without depending on them. We can work in the markets, while also building a fundamental skillset to fall back on.

And in the process, we can shrink pointless rules into irrelevance.

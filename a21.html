Conclusion

All that's left is work.

I've spun this problem around in my head for years, trying to map it out as precisely as I can.

But no matter how precise we get, there is no certainty.

We know which fundamentals to rely on. We know the inherent downsides of healthcare as a field. We understand the need for execution, and the need for strategy, and the relationship between the two.

We've mapped out the moral basis of medicine. The lack of role models, and our obligation to fix it. The necessity of earnestness, and the importance of bravely moving out into the world, despite the uncertainty.

We've capped our downside. We know how to avoid reliance on the status game — how to avoid getting trapped, where to put our attention, and when to leave the game entirely.

And in doing so, we've freed ourselves to focus on doing great work.

At this point, the riskiest thing we can do is nothing.

We're safe now. We can't be trapped. Finally, we have a chance to work as hard as we can, and see what happens. Every second we aren't doing this is a second that goes to waste.

There is no alternative. Nothing more to be gained from thinking in circles. Nobody else to blame. No more excuses. Any failure to build something incredible is entirely our fault.

What could we create, if we poured our lives into it?

I intend to find out.

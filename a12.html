Who Will Our Kids Look Up To?

Are we trapped?

Will everything decay into a demoralising status game? Or is there a better way?

Our institutions are renewed by people who choose skill over status. People who endure the repetitive drudgery of mastery, but not the repetitive drudgery of baseless popularity contests. Medicine is no different.

What's to stop us from being those people?

Arrogant laziness, for one thing. It's easy to throw the baby out with the bathwater, and dismiss the whole field as corrupt.

Mindless obedience, for another. After all, the world is much simpler if we can trust some all-knowing authority figure.

But neither of these are inevitable. We can avoid them, if we choose to.

What's the alternative? Are we going to let our children grow up in a world without hope? A world where their best shot at prosperity is tearing everybody else down?

Even if we tried, it wouldn't work. We'd go extinct. The only way forward is to take responsibility for everything, no matter how painful.

It's not a choice. Not a nice-to-have. There just isn't any other option.

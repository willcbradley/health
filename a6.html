Keep Your Awards

The world is messy.

We are imperfect, and so are our systems. For better or worse, ethical and practical constraints separate us from sources of truth.

But how much is too much? When (if ever) is it safe to insulate ourselves from reality, even slightly?

It strikes me as a one-way street.

Anybody who does real work can learn arbitrary rules, but the inverse isn't true.

If we ground ourselves in non-pointless work, we preserve all options.

Undeniable competence lets us play with real stakes, instead of purely competing for prestige, or publicity, or awards.

There's nothing wrong with these proxies, as long as we can see through the abstractions. Otherwise, how can we trust that they're worthwhile?

A More Durable Game

Suppose the medical career game rots.

Suppose it becomes so hotly contested and overpriced that the whole thing degenerates into a nepotistic bloodbath.

What then?

The obvious answer is to lean on fundamentals. But can we get any more specific than that?

Surely, there must a more concrete plan to insulate us against this kind of corruption, right?

I don't think so.

But beyond these clear-cut fundamentals, we still have morality. Nobody, no matter how influential, can take it from us.

And I suspect, on a long enough time horizon, that's all that matters.

So I'll play your game. Go ahead, rig it against me. I've made my own opportunities before, and I'll do it again.

I'll impress the people worth impressing. I'll earn the rewards worth earning. You can't touch me. You could literally kill me, and I'd still win.

Of course, you'll get what you're looking for, too. The crowd will admire you for accruing arbitrary status in an arbitrary game.

But despite your best efforts, you'll see through the facade.

And it'll eat you alive.

A Better Game

Suppose the medical career game rots.

Suppose it becomes so hotly contested and overpriced that the whole thing degenerates into a nepotistic bloodbath.

What then?

The obvious answer is to lean on fundamentals. But can we get any more specific than that?

Surely, there must a more concrete plan to insulate us against this kind of corruption, right?

I don't think so.

But beyond the fundamentals of the material world, we're still left with morality.

And nobody, no matter how influential, can take this from us.

So I'll play your game. Go ahead, rig it against me. I've made my own opportunities before, and I'll do it again.

I'll impress the people worth impressing. I'll earn the rewards worth earning. You can't touch me. Even if you literally kill me, I still win.

Of course, you'll get what you're looking for, too. The crowd will admire you for your fake accomplishments.

But if you have any sense, you'll see through the facade. You'll know, deep down, that you're a fraud.

And it'll eat you alive.

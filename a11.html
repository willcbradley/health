A More Durable Game

Suppose the medical career game rots.

Suppose it becomes so hotly contested and overpriced that the whole thing degenerates into a corrupt, demoralising bloodbath.

What then?

The obvious answer is to lean on fundamentals. But can we get any more specific than that?

Surely, there must a more concrete plan to insulate us against this threat, right?

I don't think so.

But beyond these clear-cut fundamentals, we still have morality. Nobody, no matter how influential, can take it from us.

And I suspect, on a long enough time horizon, that's all that matters.

By all means, people can rig the game against us. But they can't stop us from creating our own opportunities from scratch.

We can still impress the people worth impressing. We can still earn the rewards worth earning. They can't touch us. They could literally kill us, and we'd still win.

Of course, they'll get what they're looking for, too. The crowd will admire them for accruing arbitrary status in an arbitrary game.

But despite their best efforts, they'll see through the facade.

And it'll eat them alive.
